# Tell Me About Yourself

## Why This Question Is Asked
Interviewers ask this to:
- Understand your background
- Check communication skills
- See how well you can structure your thoughts
- Know if you are a good cultural fit

---

## How to Answer (Best Structure – 60–90 seconds)

Use this structure:
1. Present (What you are doing now)
2. Past (Education / background)
3. Future (Career goals related to the role)

---

## Sample Answer (Fresher – Software Role)

> "I am a diploma graduate in engineering and currently preparing for software placements.
> I have been learning core programming concepts, web development, and working on small projects.
> Recently, I participated in a hackathon where I worked on a web-based project.
> I enjoy learning new technologies and improving my problem-solving skills.
> I am now looking for an opportunity where I can apply my skills, learn from experienced developers,
> and grow as a software engineer."

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Speaking too long
- Sharing personal or irrelevant details
- Reading a memorized answer
- Not linking your answer to the job role

---

## Interviewer Follow-up Questions
- Tell me more about your project
- What technologies are you currently learning?
- Why should we hire you?
